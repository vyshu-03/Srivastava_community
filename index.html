History

The Srivastava community is a prominent sub-group within the Kayastha caste, traditionally known for its association with administrative and clerical professions in India. The Kayasthas have a rich history, often serving as scribes, record keepers, and officials in royal courts and under various rulers throughout Indian history. The Srivastavas, like other Kayastha sub-groups, have a strong cultural emphasis on education and professional achievement.

Famous People

Lal Bahadur Shastri

Lal Bahadur Shastri was the second Prime Minister of India. He is remembered for his leadership during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1965 and for his slogan "Jai Jawan Jai Kisan" which emphasized the importance of both soldiers and farmers. His original name was Lal Bahadur Srivastava but he earned the title "Shastri" because of his academic excellence at the ancient city of Varanasi.

Amitabh Bachchan

Amitabh Bachchan is one of the most iconic actors in Indian cinema. Known for his roles in classics like "Sholay," "Deewar," and "Zanjeer," he has had a profound impact on the Bollywood film industry. With numerous awards including the Padma Shri, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Vibhushan, Amitabh Bachchan is a global ambassador for Indian cinema and remains active in the film industry.

Harivansh Rai Bachchan

Harivansh Rai Bachchan was a celebrated Indian poet of the Hindi literary world. He is best known for his early work "Madhushala," which became a literary classic. His poems and writings are celebrated for their depth, lyrical beauty, and philosophical musings. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan for his contribution to Hindi literature and remains an influential figure in Indian poetry.
